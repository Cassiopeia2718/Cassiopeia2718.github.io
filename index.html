Trappist-1 System

Trappist-1

Trappist-1 is a red dwarf star. It is in the constellation Aquarius 40.66 light years from earth. Its radius is only slightly larger than Jupiters.

It is roughly 7.6 billion years old(around 3 billion years older than our sun). Its mass is 9% that of our sun. Given its age and mass, it can only barely support fusion in its core. Its effective tempurature is 2,566 K as apposed to our suns 5,800 K. Becuase of this, condesates can form in the photosphere(The outer layer of the star)

Planetary System

All of Trappists 7 planets orbit within 1/6 of the orbit of mercury, there orbital periods are between 1.5 to 19 days.
Becuase of the small orbital radius the planets expierience strong tidal forces compared to the earth. This tidal force streches the planets and this streching decreases the angular momentum.
